I can't speak for computing systems, since I've focused on Interactive Intelligence, but I think KBAI is a really nice introduction to the program - I think any of the David Joyner classes would be great.
But as others have mentioned, it is all based on your background, what interests you, and how much you want to be challenged. Read reviews online (like omscentral), but always with a grain of salt - everyone's skills, backgrounds, and experiences vary. I've taken KBAI, AI4R, ML4T, AI, Game AI, HCI and RL and have yet to have a negative overall experience from any course - they've all been great. Take courses you're passionate about learning about, read/prep for the prerequisites, and you should be good.
